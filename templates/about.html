ISO

ISO determines how sensitive your camera sensor is to light. A low ISO (like 100) is best for bright conditions and delivers clean images, while a high ISO (like 3200+) allows for shooting in low light but introduces noise.

Shutter Speed

Shutter speed controls how long your camera's shutter remains open. Fast speeds (1/1000s) freeze motion; slower speeds (1/30s) create motion blur and allow more light in â€” useful in low-light or creative effects.

Aperture

Aperture adjusts the size of your lens opening. A wide aperture (f/1.8) blurs the background for portraits; a narrow one (f/11) keeps more of the scene sharp â€” perfect for landscapes.
